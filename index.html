Norway-US Seafood Trade & Tariffs

Norway's seafood industry plays a significant role in its trade with the U.S., with seafood accounting for 21% of Norway’s exports to the U.S. despite only making up 3% of its total goods exports (Fish Farming Expert, 2025).

Tariff Impact on Norwegian Seafood

Historically, Norwegian salmon faced a 24% penalty duty until 2012 when the U.S. removed the restriction. In recent years, speculation over new **tariffs ranging from 10-20%** has raised concerns for Norwegian seafood exporters.

Trade Agreements & Market Access

Norway benefits from duty-free trade with the **EU** but does not enjoy the same privileges with the U.S. Its government is actively negotiating **new free trade agreements** with key global partners, including **China, India, and Mercosur** (Norwegian Seafood Council, 2025).
